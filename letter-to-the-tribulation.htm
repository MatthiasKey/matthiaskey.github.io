Overview

Letter to the Tribulation is a unique chronological reconstruction of the Book of Revelation, written from a futurist, grace-dispensational perspective. Through the use of a “cyber key,” Matthias Key decodes the order and relationship between Revelation’s spiraling visions—showing that God’s prophetic Word follows a divine architecture rather than random sequence.

The book challenges common assumptions about end-time timelines by aligning Revelation with the rest of Scripture, tracing events from the opening of the seals through the Great White Throne, and revealing how Christ’s righteousness governs every phase of the tribulation.

Key Themes

  • The true chronological order of Revelation’s judgments.
  • Understanding the “cyber key” that connects prophetic visions.
  • Distinguishing national Israel’s tribulation from the Body of Christ’s hope.
  • The rise and fall of the Beast system in a digital world.
  • The return of Christ and the restoration of divine order.

What Makes This Book Unique

Chronological Clarity

Most commentaries on Revelation follow a topical or symbolic structure. Letter to the Tribulation restores the book’s true chronological order—aligning events logically from the seals to the new creation, revealing Revelation as a divinely coded timeline.

The “Cyber Key” Framework

Matthias introduces the Cyber Key—a pattern that deciphers how Revelation’s overlapping visions interlock like a digital sequence. This perspective makes prophecy comprehensible for a modern, data-driven world.

Grace-Dividing Perspective

Written from a Pauline, right-dividing understanding of Scripture, this work distinguishes national Israel’s prophetic program from the Body of Christ’s heavenly hope— bringing doctrinal clarity often lost in traditional approaches.
